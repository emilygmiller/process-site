Determining Audience

Sticky Note Mapping

The first step in this process was to list all the groups that are directly involved with Toys for Tots, all of the groups that are peripherally involved, and all of the fans/critics of the organization. Listing these categories helped me visualize all of the possible groups that are impacted by this organization and who have a connection with the work they’re doing.

Directly Involved:
- Less fortunate children
- Less fortunate families
- Marine corps reserve
- Parents
- People who donate toys
- Financial donors
- Volunteers for collection/distribution
- Parents requesting toys
- Local community organizers
Peripherally Involved:
- More fortunate families
- Partner organizations
- Collection sites corporate sponsors
- Schools, churches, local campaigns

Next, I organized these audience groups into categories. Under the groups that were directly involved, I separated them into the categories of receiving toys, giving, and organizing.

Receiving toys:
- Parents requesting toys
- Parents
- Less fortunate families
- Less fortunate children
Giving:
- People who donate toys
- Financial donors
- More fortunate families
Organizing:
- Marine corps reserve
- Volunteers for collection/distribution
- Local community organizations
- Collection sites
- Lead of local campaigns (marine, sailor or volunteer)

The other category under peripherally relevant was “support for organization” which included the audiences of:

Receiving toys:
- Parents requesting toys
- Parents
- Less fortunate families
- Less fortunate children
Giving:
- People who donate toys
- Financial donors
- More fortunate families
Organizing:
- Marine corps reserve
- Volunteers for collection/distribution
- Local community organizations
- Collection sites
- Lead of local campaigns (marine, sailor or volunteer)

The last category was the possible critic of an audience group that I could consider:
- people who don't celebrate Christmas.



Overarching Goals

Client, Audience, Website.

Client
- Spread the joy and hope of Christmas through the gift of a new toy to less fortunate children
- Be able to donate the most toys possible to the greatest number of children in need
- Connect people with local collection sites/the local campaign in their community
- Make it easy for people to donate (financially) and donate a toy (the virtual toybox and in a physical donation site)
- Expand their outreach efforts for less fortunate children (outside of Christmas)
- Partner with other organizations to help less fortunate children in many ways
Audience
- Donate to organization (financially or through virtual toybox)
- Connect with local campaign/find a donation site in their area
- Request a toy for a child to receive
- Connect with local campaign for volunteer opportunity
- Find information about the organization (it’s history, what they do, etc.)
Website
- Make it simple for people to donate or request a toy
- Connect people with their local campaign easily
- Give people an easy/straightforward way to donate a toy virtually (virtual toybox)
- Make information about the organization easy to find and read through
- Raise awareness for other outreach efforts of the organization


Specific Goals

Putting the sticky notes in action.

User Goals Content/Action
Donors (Families, parents, churches, schools, etc.) Supporting the effort to give kids all over the joy of a new toy at Christmas that otherwise wouldn’t receive one. Donate financially online, donate through the virtual toybox, or find a local campaign to donate a physical toy in person.
Less Fortunate Families Find a way to provide their child with a new toy at Christmas that they wouldn’t be able to provide on their own. Request a toy to be received through Toys for Tots distribution campaign.
Volunteers Educating them on the need around them and getting them to appreciate the simple joy of something like a new toy through donating to those who don’t have that luxury. Information on how to volunteer in the collection and distribution campaign and on why it matters in their community.
Local Organizations in Community (Churches, schools, etc.) Facilitate the campaign of giving the greatest number of children the joy of a new toy in their community. Become a collection site for toys to be dropped off at, provide and organize volunteers.
US Marine Corps Provide awareness for the Toys for Tots campaign, grow their investment in children in communities across the country, partner with other organizations for the good of children. Clear information about organization, personal stories about the work they’re doing in communities all over, increase community interaction with organization.
